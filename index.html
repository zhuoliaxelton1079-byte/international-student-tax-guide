1 Overview of U.S. Tax Reporting

Understanding how the U.S. tax system works for international students

The U.S. tax system operates on a pay-as-you-go basis. Throughout the year, your employer or institution withholds estimated taxes from your income and sends them directly to the IRS. At year-end, you file a tax return to calculate your actual liability — you may receive a refund if too much was withheld, or owe additional tax if too little was withheld.

📅

Filing Deadline

April 15 for those with U.S. income. June 15 for Form 8843 only (no income).

📝

Your Form

Most F-1/J-1 students file Form 1040-NR (not the regular 1040).

🏛️

State Return

Wisconsin residents also file Form 1NPR for Wisconsin-source income.

📋

Form 8843

Required for ALL F-1/J-1 students, even if you had no U.S. income.

🚨
Why Filing Matters: Tax compliance is part of maintaining your visa status. Failure to file may affect future immigration applications, result in missed treaty benefits and refunds, and lead to fines, penalties, and interest if you owe taxes.

2 Tax Residency Determination Tool

Determine whether you are a Resident Alien or Nonresident Alien for federal tax purposes

ℹ️
Why This Matters: Your tax residency status determines which form you file (1040 vs. 1040-NR), what income is taxable, and what deductions/credits you can claim. Most F-1/J-1 students are nonresident aliens during their first 5 years in the U.S.
📖 Understanding the Substantial Presence Test (SPT) ▼

The Substantial Presence Test determines if you have been in the U.S. long enough to be considered a resident for tax purposes. You meet the SPT if you were present in the U.S. for:

  • At least 31 days during the current year, AND
  • At least 183 weighted days during the 3-year period, calculated as:
    • All days present in current year (2025) × 1
    • Days present in prior year (2024) × 1/3
    • Days present in two years ago (2023) × 1/6
⚠️
Exempt Individuals: F-1, J-1, M-1, and Q-1 students are exempt from counting days toward the SPT for their first 5 calendar years in the U.S. This means most international students remain nonresident aliens during this period. "Exempt" does NOT mean exempt from paying taxes!

5 Federal Return — Form 1040-NR

Step-by-step instructions for completing your U.S. federal nonresident alien tax return

🚨
Critical: You must file Form 1040-NR — NOT the regular Form 1040. When using tax software, always select "Nonresident Alien" as your filing status. Filing the wrong form can affect your visa status.
1

Confirm Your Filing Status

Select "Nonresident Alien" as your filing status in TaxSlayer. This generates Form 1040-NR. Most nonresident students file as Single. Do not select "Married Filing Jointly" unless you qualify for a rare exception.

Do NOT select "Single," "Married Filing Jointly," or any other status — you must specifically choose "Nonresident Alien."
2

Enter Personal Information

  • Full legal name (as on passport)
  • SSN or ITIN
  • Date of birth
  • U.S. mailing address you can access
  • Select "Nonresident" for state residency status
3

Enter W-2 Income

Navigate to the W-2 section and click "Begin" or "Add W-2." Copy each box exactly as shown on your W-2. Leave blank boxes empty in the software.

  • Box 1: Wages, tips, other compensation
  • Box 2: Federal income tax withheld
  • Boxes 3–6: Social Security/Medicare — should be $0 for F-1/J-1 nonresidents
  • Box 16–17: State wages and state tax withheld
💡 If you have multiple W-2 forms, add each one separately.
4

Enter 1042-S (Scholarship/Treaty Income)

Report only the taxable portion of scholarship income (housing, food, stipends). Use your school's breakdown to determine what is taxable. Tuition-only scholarships are not reported.

💡 If your employer already applied treaty benefits on your W-2 (reduced wages), do not apply them again on Schedule OI.
5

Complete Form 8843

Navigate to Federal → Miscellaneous Forms → Form 8843. This form documents your exempt days in the U.S. and is required for all F-1/J-1 students.

  • Enter your visa type and date of first entry in that status
  • Enter your passport number
  • Enter days in the U.S. that you can exclude (same count as Schedule OI)
  • Enter your school's name, address, phone, and official's name from your I-20/DS-2019
💡 When using TaxSlayer FSA, Form 8843 is e-filed with your return — no need to mail it separately!
6

Complete Schedule OI (Other Information)

Navigate to Basic Information → Schedule OI. This schedule is required for most 1040-NR filers.

  • Items A & B: Countries of citizenship and claimed residence
  • Item E: U.S. visa type on the last day of the tax year
  • Items G & H: Entry/exit dates and total days present in the U.S. over the past 3 years
  • Item L: Tax treaty exemption claims (if applicable)
💡 Check your travel history at i94.cbp.dhs.gov to get accurate entry/exit dates.
7

Deductions

Most nonresident aliens cannot claim the standard deduction. You may only itemize deductions if you have qualifying expenses (e.g., donations to U.S.-qualified charities). Students from India may claim the standard deduction for federal purposes only.

Do not claim education credits (Form 1098-T is irrelevant), earned income credits, or other credits that are unavailable to nonresidents.

6 Wisconsin State Return — Form 1NPR

Instructions for filing your Wisconsin nonresident income tax return

✅

Must File 1NPR If:

  • You earned wages for work performed in Wisconsin
  • Wisconsin-source income is $2,000 or more in 2025
  • Wisconsin income tax was withheld and you want a refund
⚠️

Important Wisconsin Rules:

  • Wisconsin taxes only Wisconsin-source income for nonresidents
  • You are always a Wisconsin Nonresident as an international student (cannot become domiciled in WI)
  • No standard deduction — even for students from India
1

Add Wisconsin State Return in TaxSlayer

After completing your federal return, select "Add State Return" and choose "Wisconsin." The software will automatically generate Form 1NPR. Do NOT file Wisconsin Form 1 (the resident return).

2

Confirm Nonresident Status

Select "Nonresident of Wisconsin" in the software. Do not choose "resident" or "part-year resident." Even if you lived in Wisconsin all year, international students are treated as Wisconsin nonresidents.

3

Enter Wisconsin W-2 Information

For each W-2, enter the Wisconsin-specific information:

  • Box 16: Wisconsin wages
  • Box 17: Wisconsin income tax withheld
  • Employer's Wisconsin state ID number
💡 Add each W-2 separately if you have more than one.
4

Adjust for Treaty Benefits (If Applicable)

If your employer reduced taxable wages for treaty benefits, confirm this in your return. If not, you may need to manually enter a negative adjustment to exclude treaty-exempt wages from Wisconsin taxable income.

⚠️ The US-India treaty does NOT apply to Wisconsin wages. Tax software may not automatically adjust for treaty benefits — verify this carefully.
5

Wisconsin Ratio Calculation

Form 1NPR taxes only Wisconsin income using the ratio: Wisconsin income ÷ Federal income. Before filing, ensure:

  • Wisconsin income includes only Wisconsin-sourced income
  • Treaty-exempt income is not included in Wisconsin taxable income
  • Interest income from regular U.S. bank accounts is not Wisconsin-source income

9 FICA Exemption & Refund (Forms 843 & 8316)

Social Security and Medicare tax exemption for nonresident alien students — and how to claim a refund if taxes were withheld in error

💡 What is FICA?

FICA (Federal Insurance Contributions Act) consists of two payroll taxes automatically withheld from wages:

🏛️

Social Security Tax

6.2% of wages (up to the annual wage base limit — $176,100 for 2025). Funds retirement and disability benefits.

🏥

Medicare Tax

1.45% of all wages (no wage cap). Funds Medicare health insurance for seniors. An additional 0.9% applies above $200,000.

⚠️ Total FICA = 7.65% of your wages. Employers match this amount. As a nonresident alien student, you are generally exempt from this tax — meaning you should take home more of your paycheck.

✅ Who Is Exempt from FICA?

Under IRC Section 3121(b)(19), nonresident alien students on certain visa types are exempt from FICA taxes. All three conditions below must be met:

📋

Condition 1: Visa Type

Must hold an F-1, J-1, M-1, or Q-1 nonimmigrant visa

🌍

Condition 2: Tax Residency

Must be a nonresident alien for U.S. tax purposes (generally the first 5 calendar years for F-1/J-1 students)

💼

Condition 3: Authorized Work

Must be performing services authorized by USCIS and consistent with the purpose of the visa

⚠️ What If FICA Was Withheld From Your Paycheck?

If you are exempt but your employer withheld Social Security or Medicare taxes, you have two options:

🏢

Option 1: Request Refund from Employer (Try First)

Contact your employer's payroll department and explain that you are a nonresident alien student exempt from FICA under IRC §3121(b)(19). Ask them to issue a corrected W-2 and refund the withheld amount.

✅ Faster and simpler — employer can correct directly on their payroll tax returns (Form 941-X).

📬

Option 2: File Forms 843 & 8316 with the IRS

If the employer refuses or is unable to provide a refund, file directly with the IRS using Form 843 (Claim for Refund) and Form 8316 (Information Regarding Request for Refund of Social Security Tax).

⏱ Takes 3–6 months — wait at least 60 days before contacting the IRS about status.

📋

Form 843 — Claim for Refund and Request for Abatement

IRS Form 843 (Rev. December 2024) | Download: irs.gov/pub/irs-pdf/f843.pdf

Form 843 is the primary IRS form used to claim a refund of Social Security and Medicare taxes that were withheld in error. Complete it carefully — errors can delay your refund.

📝 Line-by-Line Instructions for FICA Refund

1

Header — Check the Correct Box

At the top of Form 843, check the box: "A refund to an employee of social security, Medicare, or RRTA tax that was withheld in error but only if your employer will not adjust the overcollection."

2

Name & TIN

Enter your full legal name and your SSN or ITIN. If you do not have an SSN, use your ITIN. If you have neither, apply for an ITIN using Form W-7 before filing.

3

Line 1 — Tax Period

Enter the tax year for which you are claiming the refund (e.g., 01/01/2025 to 12/31/2025). File a separate Form 843 for each tax year.

4

Line 2 — Amount of Refund

Enter the total dollar amount of Social Security and/or Medicare taxes withheld in error. Find this on your W-2:

W-2 BoxDescriptionTax Rate
Box 4Social Security tax withheld6.2% of Box 3
Box 6Medicare tax withheld1.45% of Box 5

Add Box 4 + Box 6 for your total FICA withheld. Claim only your employee share (not the employer's matching portion).

5

Line 3 — Date of Payment

Enter the date(s) the taxes were paid (withheld from your paycheck). If multiple dates, list them or attach a separate sheet. You can use the pay period end dates from your pay stubs.

6

Line 4 — Type of Tax

Check Box a: "Employment" tax.

7

Line 5 — Type of Return

Check Box i: "1040" (this covers Form 1040-NR as well).

8

Line 7 — Reason for Refund

Check Box d: "Other".

9

Line 8 — Explanation

Write a clear explanation. Use this template:

"I am a nonresident alien for U.S. tax purposes under IRC §7701(b). I am present in the United States on an [F-1 / J-1] visa and have been in the U.S. for less than 5 calendar years. Under IRC §3121(b)(19), wages paid to me for services performed in the U.S. are exempt from Social Security and Medicare taxes. My employer erroneously withheld $[amount] in Social Security tax (W-2 Box 4) and $[amount] in Medicare tax (W-2 Box 6) for tax year [YYYY]. I am requesting a refund of $[total] in FICA taxes withheld in error."
10

Signature

Sign and date the form. If a paid preparer completes it, they must also sign.

⏱ Timeline & What to Expect

1

Step 1: Contact Your Employer First

Notify your employer's payroll department of the error. Provide a copy of your visa and I-20/DS-2019. Give them 2–4 weeks to respond. If they agree to refund, you are done — no IRS filing needed.

2

Step 2: Gather All Documents

Collect your W-2, visa copy, I-94 printout, I-20 or DS-2019, and any employer statement. Make photocopies of everything — keep originals.

3

Step 3: Complete Forms 843 and 8316

Download both forms from IRS.gov. Complete Form 843 first, then Form 8316. Use the line-by-line instructions above. Write your explanation on Line 8 of Form 843.

4

Step 4: Mail to IRS

Mail the completed forms with all attachments to the Ogden, UT address via certified mail. Keep the tracking number and a copy of everything sent.

5

Step 5: Wait 60–180 Days

The IRS typically takes 3 to 6 months to process FICA refund claims. Do not contact the IRS until at least 60 days have passed. You can check refund status by calling 1-800-829-1040 after 60 days.

6

Step 6: Receive Refund Check

The IRS will mail a refund check to the address on your Form 843. If your address changes, file Form 8822 to update it. The refund is not taxable income — do not report it on your next year's return.

🏛️ Wisconsin State Note on FICA

Wisconsin does not impose its own Social Security or Medicare tax — FICA is a federal-only tax. The FICA exemption and refund process described above applies only to federal taxes. Your Wisconsin Form 1NPR is not affected by FICA withholding errors. However, if you receive a FICA refund in a future year, it is not considered Wisconsin taxable income.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Can I claim a FICA refund for multiple years at once?

No. You must file a separate Form 843 for each tax year. However, you can mail multiple Form 843s in the same envelope. The statute of limitations is 3 years from the return due date or 2 years from the payment date, whichever is later.

My employer says they already paid the FICA taxes to the IRS. Can I still get a refund?

Yes. Even if your employer has already remitted the taxes to the IRS, you can still file Form 843 and Form 8316 to claim your employee's share directly from the IRS. The employer's portion is a separate matter between the employer and the IRS.

I don't have an SSN — can I still file for a FICA refund?

Yes, but you need a Taxpayer Identification Number. Apply for an ITIN (Form W-7) if you are not eligible for an SSN. Once you receive your ITIN, use it on Form 843 and Form 8316. Note: You cannot claim a FICA refund without a TIN.

Does the FICA refund affect my 1040-NR income tax return?

No. FICA taxes (Social Security and Medicare) are separate from federal income tax. The Form 843/8316 refund process is completely independent of your 1040-NR filing. Do not include FICA refund claims on your 1040-NR. File them separately.

I became a resident alien during the year. Am I exempt for the whole year?

No. The FICA exemption only applies during the period when you were a nonresident alien. If you became a resident alien partway through the year (e.g., you passed the Substantial Presence Test), FICA applies from that date forward. You can only claim a refund for the portion of the year when you were a nonresident alien and FICA was withheld in error.

Can UWGB VITA help me file Forms 843 and 8316?

VITA programs primarily assist with income tax returns (Form 1040-NR). Forms 843 and 8316 are refund claims, not income tax returns, so they fall outside the standard VITA scope. However, VITA volunteers can provide guidance on the process. For complex situations, consult a qualified tax professional or contact the IRS directly at 1-800-829-1040.
